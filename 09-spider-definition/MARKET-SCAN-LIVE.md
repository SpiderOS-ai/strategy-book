### Frontier Platform Details

### Strategy Impact: MEDIUM-HIGH

OpenAI is playing the enterprise game. Their pricing and go-to-market exclude SMBs. But their "open platform" approach (managing non-OpenAI agents) is a strategic signal: the orchestration layer matters more than the model layer.

## 7. SAASPOCALYPSE

### THIS IS THE BIGGEST MACRO STORY OF THE MONTH.

### The Numbers

- **$1 TRILLION+** in market cap erased from software stocks in first 6 weeks of 2026
- **IGV (iShares Software ETF)** down 24.6% YTD (vs. broader tech down 5.8%)
- **"Black Tuesday" (Feb 3)**: Anthropic's Cowork launch triggered $285B single-day selloff
- **"Software-mageddon" (week of Feb 9)**: S&P 500 Software Index dropped 13% in 5 trading sessions, wiping $800B
- **IPO market frozen**: Software sector plunge has frozen the IPO pipeline

### The Root Cause: Death of the Seat-Based Model

The traditional "per seat" pricing model is dying. The market is repricing every SaaS company based on a brutal new math:

> If one AI agent can replace the output of five humans, the total addressable market for seat-based software shrinks by 80%.

Companies are shifting from selling software licenses to selling outcomes and AI agent actions. This is not a temporary correction -- it's a structural shift.

### The Trigger Event

Anthropic's Claude Cowork demonstrated that an AI agent could:
- Navigate complex enterprise software
- Execute legal contract reviews
- Manage financial triage
- ALL without human oversight

This made the theoretical "agents replace seats" argument concrete and visible.

### Forrester's Take

> "SaaS as we know it is dead."

Forrester has declared the transition from the "SaaS Era" to the "Agentic Era" official.

### Why It Matters for Spider

**THIS IS SPIDER'S ENTIRE THESIS PLAYING OUT IN REAL TIME.**

The SaaSpocalypse is the market validating everything Spider has been building toward. The old model (buy 10 SaaS seats per employee) is breaking. The new model (deploy AI agents that do the work) is emerging. Spider is positioned to be the platform that helps SMBs make this transition.

But there's a nuance: the SaaSpocalypse is also a WARNING. If Spider prices on a per-seat basis, it will face the same structural pressure. Spider must price on outcomes/value, not seats.

### Strategy Impact: CRITICAL -- EXISTENTIAL VALIDATION

This is the single most important macro signal for Spider. The market is moving FROM what we're replacing TO what we're building.
