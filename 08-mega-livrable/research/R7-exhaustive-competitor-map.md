## 14. Collision Risk Analysis (18-Month Horizon)

*Which actors are most likely to enter Spider's exact space (AI-native operational intelligence for European PMEs) by August 2027?*

### TIER 1: Highest Collision Risk (>50% probability of entering Spider's space)

**Dust.tt**
- **Current trajectory:** Hiring Forward Deployed Engineers, targeting 5x growth, Sequoia-backed.
- **Why collision is likely:** French DNA, AI agent platform narrative, enterprise momentum. If Dust decides to build an "Odoo integration" or "PME tier," it directly competes.
- **Why collision might NOT happen:** Dust is moving upmarket + US-first. Their ICP is tech companies (Clay, Cursor, Persona), not traditional PMEs using Odoo/Excel.
- **Probability:** 30-40% in 18 months (low for downmarket move, high for narrative capture).

**Pennylane**
- **Current trajectory:** EUR175M raised, 800K clients, approaching profitability, building financial OS.
- **Why collision is likely:** Same customer (French PME via accountant channel). If Pennylane adds AI-powered operational insights beyond accounting, it becomes Spider.
- **Why collision might NOT happen:** Pennylane is accounting-first. Expanding into operational intelligence requires a fundamentally different product DNA.
- **Probability:** 25-35% in 18 months (accounting expansion is slow and regulated).

**n8n**
- **Current trajectory:** $2.5B valuation, $180M raised, open-source workflows, adding AI agents.
- **Why collision is likely:** European, developer-friendly, already has "AI Agent" capabilities. One step from "workflow automation" to "business intelligence automation."
- **Why collision might NOT happen:** n8n is developer-first and horizontal. Building domain-specific PME operational intelligence is not in their DNA.
- **Probability:** 20-30% in 18 months.

### TIER 2: Moderate Collision Risk (20-40% probability)

**Odoo (via AI features in Odoo 19/20)**
- Odoo adding "Odoo AI" features is inevitable. The question is whether they build deep operational intelligence or just chatbot-level AI.
- **Probability:** 25% for meaningful AI analytics; 70% for superficial AI features that don't compete.

**Agicap**
- Cash flow intelligence could expand to full financial operations intelligence.
- **Probability:** 20% in 18 months (they are focused on treasury, not broad operations).

**Zapier / Make**
- Both adding AI agent capabilities. Could build "AI-powered business intelligence" on top of workflow data.
- **Probability:** 15-20% each (horizontal focus, not PME-specific).

### TIER 3: Low Collision Risk (<20% probability)

| Actor | Why It Could Happen | Why It Probably Won't | Probability |
|-------|--------------------|-----------------------|-------------|
| Relevance AI | Same "AI workforce" narrative | Australian, no European PME focus | 10% |
| LayerX | Back-office AI automation | Enterprise-only, no SMB tier | 10% |
| Beam AI | SMB AI agents | Too early stage, US-focused | 10% |
| Adept AI | UI-based automation could serve PMEs | $415M enterprise focus, no PME intent | 5% |
| Power BI | Could add AI operational intelligence | Microsoft is enterprise-focused, won't build PME-specific ops | 5% |
| Salesforce Agentforce | "Small Business" tier | Salesforce never successfully served true SMBs | 5% |

### Summary: Spider's 18-Month Window

Spider has a **clear 18-month window** where no single competitor is building "Palantir for PME" -- the specific combination of:
1. AI-native operational intelligence (not just BI or automation)
2. Built on top of existing ERP/tools (Odoo-first)
3. Targeting European PMEs (2M-50M EUR)
4. Managed deployment model (FDE-inspired)
5. Tacit knowledge capture as a moat

The biggest risk is not a single competitor but **the "good enough" stack**: a PME stitching together Pennylane (accounting) + Agicap (cash) + Upflow (AR) + Metabase (dashboards) + Zapier (automation) = 80% of what Spider offers, without the unified intelligence layer. Spider's pitch must make the integrated value undeniably superior.
