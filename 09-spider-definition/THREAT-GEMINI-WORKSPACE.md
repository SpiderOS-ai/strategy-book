## 1. WHAT GEMINI CAN DO IN WORKSPACE TODAY

### February 2026 -- The Current State

Gemini is no longer a bolt-on chatbot. It is now **deeply integrated into every Google Workspace app** as a native AI layer. Here is the full inventory of capabilities:

#### 1.1 Gmail -- AI-Powered Inbox

- **Auto-sorting by urgency:** Bills, deadlines, and client emails on top. Newsletters and promotions tucked away.
- **Thread summarization:** Long email chains summarized automatically.
- **Draft responses:** Gemini drafts context-aware replies.
- **AI Overviews in Gmail:** Powered by Gemini 3, Gmail now provides AI-generated overviews of email threads and search results.
- **Labeling and triage:** Automated labeling (e.g., "If an email contains a question for me, label it 'To respond' and ping me in Chat").

**Spider implication:** A French PME using Gmail already has its emails read, categorized, and summarized by Gemini. Spider's "People" module (relationship graph from Gmail) faces a native competitor that is already running in the same inbox.

#### 1.2 Google Sheets -- AI Data Analysis

- **Gemini side panel in Sheets:** Ask questions about your data in natural language.
- **Formula generation:** Describe what you want, Gemini writes the formula.
- **Data analysis:** "What are the trends in column B over the last 6 months?"
- **Table creation from prompts:** Describe a data structure, Gemini creates it.

**Spider implication:** Spider's core "data-crossing" value prop (analyzing Jordan's 726 campaign lines to find the 39.3% unpaid invoices pattern) is something Gemini can approximate if the data is already in Sheets.

#### 1.3 Google Calendar -- AI Scheduling

- **Smart scheduling:** Search everyone's availability, suggest optimal times.
- **Productivity Planner Gem:** Brings together information from Gmail, Calendar, and Drive to provide an overview of urgent emails and calendar events for the week.
- **Cross-app awareness:** The calendar assistant knows about your emails and Drive documents.

#### 1.4 Google Docs -- AI Writing and Analysis

- **"Help Me Write"** for content generation.
- **Gemini side panel** for document summarization, rewriting, and Q&A about document content.
- **Cross-referencing:** Can pull context from Drive files while writing.

#### 1.5 Google Slides and Vids -- AI Presentation

- **Original image generation** for presentations.
- **Google Vids:** Create polished videos from text prompts or existing slides.
- **Custom 30-second soundtracks** generated by Lyria 3.

#### 1.6 Google Meet -- AI Meetings

- **Ask Gemini in Meet:** Available to Business Standard customers (expanded January 2026).
- **Real-time speech translation** that maintains tone and voice.
- **Meeting summaries and action items.**

#### 1.7 Google Drive -- AI Search and Organization

- **Semantic search** across all files.
- **Cross-app context:** Gemini understands relationships between documents.

### THE GAME CHANGER: Google Workspace Studio (GA February 2026)

This is the critical development. Workspace Studio is **not** just Gemini chatting inside apps. It is an **agentic AI platform** for building autonomous workflows:

- **Natural language agent creation:** "Simply describe what you want to automate and Gemini 3 will create a flow for it."
- **Cross-app automation:** Agents can work across Gmail, Drive, Calendar, Sheets, Docs, and Chat in a single workflow.
- **Autonomous actions:** Agents can draft replies, add to documents, extract information, label emails, create follow-up tasks.
- **Pre-meeting briefs:** Auto-generated from Gmail + Calendar + Drive context.
- **Always-on workflows:** Not just one-shot -- continuous monitoring and action.
- **Pre-built connectors:** Salesforce, Jira, Asana, Mailchimp, Slack.
- **Custom API integration:** Webhooks + Apps Script for connecting to any external service.
- **Maximum 20 steps per agent.**
- **Available with Business Standard ($14/user/month) and above.**
- **End user access rolling out gradually starting February 2026** -- literally right now.

**Spider implication: THIS IS THE THREAT.** Workspace Studio is not "Gemini answers questions." It is "Gemini does things." It can read emails, update sheets, create docs, send notifications, and orchestrate across apps -- all from a natural language description. This is directly competitive with Spider's core automation value prop.

### MCP Support -- The Open Protocol Play

Google announced **official MCP (Model Context Protocol) support** for all Google and Google Cloud services:

- Fully managed, remote MCP servers.
- Developers can point AI agents or MCP clients to a globally-consistent endpoint.
- **Apigee integration** allows customers to expose their own APIs and third-party APIs as discoverable tools for agents.
- Available in Python and JavaScript SDKs (experimental).

**Spider implication:** MCP support means Gemini can theoretically connect to ANY tool that exposes an MCP server -- including, eventually, tools like Pennylane or Odoo if they build MCP servers. This erodes Spider's "connector" advantage over time.

### Gemini Enterprise -- The Heavy-Duty Version

For larger companies, Google also offers **Gemini Enterprise** (separate from Workspace):

- **Agent Designer:** No-code/low-code platform for multi-step agents.
- **Cross-platform connectors:** Salesforce, SAP, Box, OpenText, ServiceNow, Workday.
- **Custom business process automation.**

This is not relevant for most French PMEs (too expensive, too complex), but it signals Google's direction: full business process orchestration.

## 2. WHAT GEMINI CANNOT DO (THE GAPS)

### Gap 1: NO connection to French business tools (Pennylane, Odoo, French banks)

This is the single most important gap.

**Gemini Workspace Studio connectors as of February 2026:**
- Google Workspace apps (native)
- Salesforce, Jira, Asana, Mailchimp, Slack (pre-built)
- Webhooks + Apps Script (custom)

**What is NOT in the connector list:**
- **Pennylane** (no integration, no connector, no MCP server)
- **Odoo** (third-party Odoo modules exist for Gemini API, but NOT native Workspace Studio connectors)
- **French banking APIs** (no Bankin', no Bridge, no direct bank connections)
- **French payroll tools** (no Payfit, no Silae)
- **French legal/compliance tools** (no LegalPlace, no Captain Contrat)

A French PME that uses Pennylane for accounting, Odoo for operations, and Gmail for email CANNOT get Gemini to cross-reference these systems. Gemini sees the Gmail side. It does not see the Pennylane invoices. It does not see the Odoo purchase orders. It cannot calculate that 39.3% of invoices are unpaid because it has no access to the invoicing data.

**This gap is temporary.** Pennylane or Odoo could build MCP servers or Workspace Studio connectors. Third parties could build bridges via webhooks. But as of February 2026, this gap is REAL and STRUCTURAL.

### Gap 2: NO business process understanding (no ontology, no "process fingerprint")

Gemini can summarize emails. It can analyze spreadsheet data. But it does NOT:

- **Map business processes** from conversations (Spider's "speech-to-process")
- **Build an ontology** of the business (entities, relationships, rules)
- **Accumulate contextual knowledge** about HOW a specific business operates over time
- **Understand that "when Schmidt Bayeux gets a reminder on Day+15, they pay faster"** -- this requires months of observation and pattern detection across email + invoicing data

Gemini operates on what is currently in front of it (the current email, the current spreadsheet). It does not build a persistent, evolving model of the business.

**Nuance:** Gemini IS getting better at cross-app context (the Productivity Planner Gem combines Gmail + Calendar + Drive). And Workspace Studio agents can be "always-on." But there is no equivalent of Spider's proposed Claims Engine or cumulative data intelligence.

### Gap 3: NO proactive alerting from cross-system analysis

Gemini can tell you "here's a summary of your unread emails." It CANNOT tell you:

- "Client X has not paid 3 invoices totaling 15K EUR and their last email tone has shifted negative -- risk of default."
- "Your cash flow will be short 37K EUR in July because of the seasonal peak, based on the pattern from last year."
- "Your margin on LED campaigns is 2x higher than 2m2 but you're allocating 70% of time to 2m2."

These insights require **crossing data from multiple systems** (email sentiment + invoicing data + calendar patterns + spreadsheet financials). Gemini only sees the Google Workspace side.

### Gap 4: NO human-in-the-loop deployment model

Google's model: Here is a tool. Configure it yourself. Good luck.

Spider's model (as designed): A human (Nathan, then a FDE) comes in, spends 2h30 understanding your business, builds a prototype, maps your processes, and deploys a customized system.

For a French PME patron who is **not tech-savvy** (France Num: 61% of French PMEs lack digital competencies), the difference between "here is a tool, build your own agents" and "we do it for you" is the difference between adoption and abandonment.

Workspace Studio requires the user to:
1. Understand what can be automated
2. Describe it in natural language
3. Test the agent
4. Debug when it fails
5. Maintain it over time

A PME patron in Caen running an outdoor advertising company is not going to do this.

### Gap 5: NO industry-specific intelligence

Gemini is a horizontal tool. It does not know that:
- In French outdoor advertising, commissions are 13-15% for independent networks
- The seasonal peak is September (back-to-school campaigns)
- Cadres Blancs and JCDecaux have different commission structures
- Municipal contracts in Normandy have specific regulatory requirements

Spider's ontology templates (if built) would encode this knowledge for reuse across clients in the same vertical. Gemini has no equivalent.

### Gap 6: 20-step limit on Workspace Studio agents

Workspace Studio agents are capped at 20 steps. Complex business workflows (receive invoice -> match to PO -> check delivery -> flag discrepancy -> notify manager -> update accounting -> schedule payment) often require more than 20 steps. This limit constrains the complexity of what can be automated.

### Gap 7: No loops in Workspace Studio (as of February 2026)

The documentation notes that "loops and service accounts" are known limitations expected to be addressed later in 2026. This means agents cannot yet do iterative processes like "for each unpaid invoice older than 30 days, send a customized reminder."

- Younger companies

## 5. WHERE SPIDER STILL WINS (IF ANYWHERE)

### Win 1: Cross-system data intelligence (THE MAIN ONE)

Gemini sees Google. Spider sees Google + Pennylane + Odoo + banks + CRM + everything else.

**The "Jordan test":**
- **Gemini can:** Summarize Jordan's emails, analyze his campaign spreadsheet, schedule his meetings.
- **Gemini cannot:** Tell Jordan that 39.3% of his invoices are unpaid, that Schmidt Bayeux pays faster with Day+15 reminders, that the September peak requires 37K EUR pre-committed in July, that LED campaigns have 2x the margin of 2m2.
- **Spider can** (theoretically): All of the above, because it connects to Pennylane (invoices), Notion (campaigns), and Gmail (communications) simultaneously.

**This is a real advantage TODAY.** It is a temporary advantage. As Pennylane/Odoo build more integrations and MCP servers become standard, the cross-system gap will narrow. Spider has a **12-24 month window** where this advantage is strong.

### Win 2: Human-deployed, not self-service

The PME patron does not build agents. Spider builds agents FOR them.

**The "France Num reality":**
- 61% of French PMEs lack digital competencies
- These patrons will not open Workspace Studio and write "If an email contains a question, label it as 'To respond'"
- They WILL say "yes" when Nathan shows them a prototype that saved Jordan 15 hours/week

**This is a go-to-market advantage, not a product advantage.** Google could hire partners to deploy Workspace Studio for PMEs. But as of today, Google's model is self-service + online help. Spider's model is white-glove deployment.

### Win 3: Process intelligence (if the Claims Engine gets built)

Gemini analyzes data that is in front of it. Spider (in theory) builds a cumulative model of how the business operates:
- Process fingerprinting
- Ontology templates per vertical
- Cross-temporal pattern detection (6 months of data reveals patterns that one day of data cannot)

**This is the strongest theoretical moat. But it does not exist yet.** The Claims Engine is a design doc. The ontology is a concept. The process fingerprint is an idea. If Spider does not BUILD these in the next 12 months, this advantage evaporates.

### Win 4: Industry vertical knowledge

Spider (if it serves 5+ clients in outdoor advertising) would know:
- Commission structures by network
- Seasonal patterns by region
- Supplier reliability ratings
- Municipal contract specificities

Gemini is horizontal. It knows nothing about the outdoor advertising industry in Normandy.

**This advantage only materializes if Spider achieves density in a vertical.** With 0 paying clients today, it is a future advantage, not a current one.

### Win 5: The M&A platform vision (Phase 3)

Spider's long-term vision is to become an M&A intelligence platform -- understanding businesses so deeply that it can facilitate acquisitions. Gemini has no equivalent ambition. This is a differentiation that matters in 3-5 years, not today.

### Win 6: Data sovereignty / European compliance

Spider could position as a European-first, RGPD-native solution. Google is American. Data goes to Google servers. Some French PMEs (especially those working with public sector or defense) care about this. Most do not.

## 6. HONEST ASSESSMENT: IS THIS FATAL FOR SPIDER?

### The Brutal Truth

**No, it is not fatal. But it fundamentally constrains Spider's positioning.**

Here is why:

#### What Gemini kills for Spider:

1. **"We automate your Gmail"** -- Dead. Gemini does this natively.
2. **"We summarize your emails"** -- Dead. Gemini does this natively.
3. **"We analyze your spreadsheets"** -- Dead. Gemini does this natively.
4. **"We manage your calendar"** -- Dead. Gemini does this natively.
5. **"We build agents for your Workspace"** -- Dying. Workspace Studio is launching right now.

Any Spider value proposition that starts with "we do X with your Google Workspace data" is competing with a feature that is **already included in the customer's existing subscription at no additional cost.**

#### What Gemini does NOT kill for Spider:

1. **"We cross your Pennylane invoices with your Gmail communications to detect unpaid patterns"** -- ALIVE. Gemini cannot do this.
2. **"We map your entire business process from a conversation"** -- ALIVE. Gemini cannot do this.
3. **"We connect ALL your tools (not just Google) into one intelligence layer"** -- ALIVE. Gemini cannot do this.
4. **"We deploy it for you. You do nothing."** -- ALIVE. Google does not do this.
5. **"After 6 months, we know your business better than you do"** -- ALIVE (if built). Gemini has no persistent business model.

### The Strategic Implication

Spider CANNOT be "AI for your Google Workspace." That market is taken. By Google. For free.

Spider MUST be "the intelligence layer that connects everything Google DOESN'T see."

**The revised pitch:**

> "Google Gemini is great. It reads your emails and organizes your calendar. But it doesn't see your Pennylane invoices. It doesn't see your Odoo orders. It doesn't see your bank statements. It doesn't know that Client X owes you 47K EUR and their last 3 emails have been increasingly hostile. Spider sees EVERYTHING -- not just your Google apps, but your accounting, your operations, your cash flow, your client relationships. Gemini tells you what's in your inbox. Spider tells you what's happening in your BUSINESS."

### The Time Pressure

This advantage is **time-limited:**

- **12 months** before Pennylane likely builds its own AI features or MCP server
- **18 months** before Odoo 20 ships with agentic AI (announced for September 2026)
- **24 months** before the MCP ecosystem makes cross-tool integration commoditized

Spider has a **12-24 month window** to:
1. Build the cross-system connectors that Gemini does not have
2. Accumulate enough client data to create a process intelligence moat
3. Develop the Claims Engine / ontology that gives Spider persistent business understanding
4. Sign enough clients to create vertical knowledge templates

If Spider does not execute in this window, Gemini + Pennylane AI + Odoo AI + MCP connectors will collectively cover 80% of what Spider promises. And they will be cheaper.

### The Final Verdict

| Dimension | Threat Level | Time Horizon |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Email/Calendar automation | FATAL -- do not compete here | Already lost |
| Spreadsheet analysis | HIGH -- Gemini is native, free | 6 months |
| Cross-app automation (Google only) | HIGH -- Workspace Studio launching now | 3-6 months |
| Cross-SYSTEM intelligence (Google + Pennylane + Odoo + banks) | LOW today, MEDIUM in 18 months | 12-24 month window |
| Business process mapping/ontology | LOW -- Gemini does not do this | 24+ months |
| Human-deployed white-glove service | LOW -- Google does not do this | 36+ months |
| Industry vertical knowledge | LOW -- Gemini is horizontal | 24+ months |
| Persistent business intelligence (Claims Engine) | LOW -- but only if Spider BUILDS it | N/A until built |

**Gemini is not an existential threat to Spider IF Spider pivots its positioning away from "AI for your productivity apps" toward "AI that understands your entire business." If Spider tries to compete on email summarization, spreadsheet analysis, or basic automation inside Google apps, it is dead before it starts.**

The question is not "can Spider beat Gemini?" (it cannot, and should not try). The question is "can Spider build something Gemini structurally cannot?" The answer is yes -- cross-system business intelligence with human deployment. But the window is closing, and the clock started in February 2026.
