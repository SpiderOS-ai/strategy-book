# Evolution of the Spider / Drakkar Equity Deal

> **Author:** Nathan Menard
> **Date:** February 2026
> **Nature:** Documents how the Spider/Drakkar equity deal evolved from initial proposal to final structure
> **Reliability:** High -- factual record of cap table negotiations
> **Key context:**
> - Drakkar Group cap table: Nathan 47%, Jean Le Tulzo 38%, Thierry Menard 15%
> - Drakkar owns: 100% Studio (in transfer), 87.5% Accelerateur (Nicolas Guimier 12.5%), 100% Academie
> - Emotional trigger: CODIR Jan 31 2026 where Jean cracked -- wanted to be a builder, not a spectator
> - Final Spider proposal: Nathan 51%, Jean 20%, Remy 14%, Thierry 10%, Drakkar 5%

---

## Part 1: The Initial Deal

The structure Nathan originally had in mind:

| Shareholder | % Spider | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nathan | ~70% | Founder, majority control |
| Remy | ~10% | CTO, technical co-founder |
| Drakkar | 20% | Invested 100k EUR |

### Rationale for this structure

- **Spider = Nathan + Remy's project.** They built it from the beginning. It is their story, not Drakkar's.
- **Clean cap table for fundraising.** If VCs come in, Nathan raises under his own name. He wants a cap table that signals clearly: majority founder, technical CTO, no corporate mess.
- **Maintain control.** Nathan has a visceral need to define his own rules. Dropping below 50% on Drakkar when Thierry entered had "broken" him emotionally -- even if it was rationally sound.
- **Drakkar = financing vehicle.** Jean and Thierry would touch Spider only indirectly through their Drakkar shares (~8% and ~3% respectively). They finance, they benefit from the service-as-software upside, but they do not steer.

### What this implied for Jean and Thierry

- No operational role in Spider
- No direct equity
- They watch from the stands

---

## Part 2: What Made Everything Shift

### Context: CODIR (Friday, January 31, 2026)

Three negotiations running simultaneously:

1. **Drakkar shareholders' agreement** -- governance, veto rights, decision-making
2. **OBO (Owner Buy-Out)** -- cash-out of 200-300k EUR, salary restructuring
3. **Spider structure**

Tensions had accumulated over six months: Nathan called Jean less, gave him less attention, worked on Spider solo with Remy. Jean kept handling the operational mess (CET, Zalina, Nadine, Nico) while Nathan innovated.

### The tipping point with Jean

Nathan presents the Spider deal: "Drakkar invests 100k EUR for 20%."

Jean does not contest the numbers. He asks a deeper question: **"Why isn't Spider just Drakkar?"**

Nathan answers:

- "It's been the project with Remy from the start, it's not the Drakkar story."
- "If we need to raise, I raise under my name -- I need a clean cap table."

What Jean hears:

- "The most exciting project -- you don't get to touch it."
- "You finance, you watch."
- "The next goal is mine, not ours."

**Jean cracks. He cries.** Not about the money -- about meaning.

He says, in substance:

> "I played defender for 6 years so you could score. Fine. But now you're telling me the next match is in a different stadium, and I'm left sweeping the locker room of the old one."

He adds:

> "Originally, I'm a striker. I chose defense because it served the team. But now I see the builder, the modules, the finance piece -- I want to work on it, structure it, confront it with clients."

### Nathan's realization (not verbalized in the moment)

While explaining the "service-as-software" model to Jean, Nathan realizes his in-between position is flawed. He says: "Spider is a massive lever for Drakkar" -- but then why isn't Drakkar truly part of it?

He realizes:

- If he truly wants to build the Palantir of SMBs, he needs the consulting brick.
- The consulting brick = the Forward Deployed Engineers.
- The FDEs = Thierry (industrial credibility, senior selling) + Jean (structuring, client relationships).
- Without them directly on the cap table, they will never be truly all-in.

### The mentor's diagnosis

The mentor told Nathan:

> "You're building options, not an empire. Spider as proposed isn't a startup -- it's a personal life-insurance policy in disguise. You're asking your partners to finance your optionality while you keep 70%. Jean is right to be disturbed."

And:

> "The control paradox is destroying you. You want a unicorn but you refuse dilution. Unicorns aren't built by people who keep their options open. They're built by people who burn everything else."

## Part 3: The Evolution of Nathan's Thinking

### Discussion with Remy (Sunday, February 2, 2026)

Nathan calls Remy to test a new structure before presenting it to Jean and Thierry.

He explains:

> "I had a huge discussion with Jean. He made me realize something. My initial structure created exactly what he feared: a side-project where he finances without being part of it."

Nathan lays out his new vision:

- **Spider = truly all-in**, not a hedge
- **Objective = raise within 12-18 months**
- **Palantir model:** tech brick (Nathan + Remy) + consulting/FDE brick (Jean + Thierry)
- **Long-term:** potentially SF for the raise, US capital, European market

Nathan proposes to Remy:

> "Drakkar takes 5% (just enough for the parent-subsidiary tax regime and to finance). Thierry 10% direct. Jean 20% direct. Me 51%. And you, I'm bringing you up to 14%."

**Remy validates.** He understands that what they are buying with Jean and Thierry is the consulting piece -- the part he does not want to manage.

## Part 4: The Final Proposed Deal

### Spider cap table -- final structure

| Shareholder | % | Role | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nathan | 51% | Vision, product, fundraising | Lead founder, raises under his name |
| Jean | 20% | CFO, offer structuring, ops | Co-founder of consulting brick, front-line client |
| Remy | 14% | CTO, infrastructure | Builds the tech -- no product without him |
| Thierry | 10% | Lead FDE, scale consulting | Industrial credibility, senior sales |
| Drakkar | 5% | Financing (100k EUR) | Parent-subsidiary tax regime, corporate link |

### Comparison: initial deal vs. final deal

| Shareholder | Initial Deal | Final Deal | Delta |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nathan | ~70% | 51% | -19 pts |
| Jean | ~8% (indirect via Drakkar) | 20% (direct) | +12 pts |
| Thierry | ~3% (indirect via Drakkar) | 10% (direct) | +7 pts |
| Remy | 10% | 14% | +4 pts |
| Drakkar | 20% | 5% | -15 pts |

**What Nathan sacrifices:** 19 points of dilution before a single euro of revenue.

**What Nathan buys:** Alignment. Jean and Thierry go from spectators to co-founders. They have a reason to fight.

---

## Part 5: The Two-Phase Mechanism

### Phase 1: Incubation (12-18 months) -- Zero risk

- Drakkar finances (100k EUR)
- Drakkar's commercial force signs the first clients
- Jean structures the consulting offers
- Thierry ensures operational continuity at Drakkar
- Nathan + Remy build the tech
- All service-as-software revenue stays at Drakkar

### Phase 2: Fundraising

- Seed round: 2-5M EUR (Europe or US)
- At that point, Jean and Thierry face a choice:
  - **Option A:** Sell Drakkar, join Spider full-time
  - **Option B:** Stay at Drakkar, get diluted on Spider

Nathan's intuition: if Spider takes off, nobody will want to stay at Drakkar.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

### The three-act evolution

1. **Initial structure (pre-Jan 31):** Nathan holds ~70%, Remy ~10%, Drakkar 20%. Spider is Nathan's personal project with Remy. Jean and Thierry are passive beneficiaries through Drakkar. Clean cap table for VC, maximum founder control.

2. **Confrontation (CODIR Jan 31, 2026):** Jean breaks down emotionally. He refuses to be a spectator financing Nathan's optionality. The mentor diagnoses Nathan's "control paradox" -- wanting a unicorn while refusing dilution. Nathan realizes the Palantir model requires a consulting brick that only Jean and Thierry can deliver.

3. **Final structure (validated Feb 2, 2026):** Nathan 51%, Jean 20%, Remy 14%, Thierry 10%, Drakkar 5%. Every person holds direct equity. Alignment replaces control as the organizing principle.

### Why each person gets their share

| Shareholder | Why this % |
|---|---|
| **Nathan (51%)** | Lead founder, product visionary, will raise funds under his name. Keeps majority to maintain strategic control -- the non-negotiable floor after the trauma of losing majority on Drakkar. |
| **Jean (20%)** | The biggest shift. Went from ~8% indirect to 20% direct. Earns it as the co-founder of the consulting/FDE brick: CFO, offer structuring, front-line client relationships. His emotional breakdown proved his commitment is real -- he wants to build, not watch. |
| **Remy (14%)** | CTO who builds the core technology. Bumped from 10% to 14% as recognition that the product does not exist without him. Validates the new structure because Jean and Thierry handle the consulting work he does not want to do. |
| **Thierry (10%)** | Lead Forward Deployed Engineer. Brings industrial credibility and senior sales capability. Moved from ~3% indirect to 10% direct. Essential for the Palantir-style consulting model to function at scale. |
| **Drakkar (5%)** | Reduced from 20% to 5%. Retains just enough for the parent-subsidiary tax regime (regime mere-fille) and to channel the initial 100k EUR financing. No longer a dominant vehicle -- becomes a support structure. |

### The core trade

Nathan gave up 19 points of personal equity before earning a single euro. In return, he converted three stakeholders from spectators into co-founders with direct skin in the game. The bet: aligned founders building together will create far more value than a solo founder hedging with a corporate shell.

> **In one sentence:** Nathan went from "Spider = my personal hedge, Drakkar finances and watches" to "Spider = our second leg, we go all-in together, and the next goal is ours."
