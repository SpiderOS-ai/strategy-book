# Palantir Government Contract Details: Structure, Pricing, SOW Language & Deliverables

## Research Date: February 17, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

This report documents the concrete details of Palantir Technologies' government contracts as visible through public procurement databases, parliamentary records, G-Cloud frameworks, and investigative journalism. The goal is to understand what Palantir actually promises to deliver -- the Statement of Work (SOW) language, pricing structures, deliverable specifications, and contractual patterns that define how the company operates in the public sector. Palantir's government revenue reached $1.57 billion in 2024 (54.8% of total revenue), with USAspending.gov showing federal obligations growing from $4.4 million in 2009 to $970.5 million in 2025. The company has received more than $1.9 billion in U.S. federal contracts since 2008, with the Department of Defense accounting for at least $1.65 billion of that total.

The G-Cloud pricing documents reveal that Palantir's professional services are **not priced by the hour or by FDE headcount** in the standard framework. Instead, they use **per-person-per-quarter** for implementation engineering and **per-two-week-sprint** for solution design. This is a deliberate abstraction away from time-and-materials consulting, positioning the deliverable as outcome/sprint-based rather than labor-hour-based.

## 3. NHS Federated Data Platform -- The GBP 330M Contract

### Contract Overview

In November 2023, NHS England awarded a Palantir-led consortium a contract to build the Federated Data Platform (FDP), valued at approximately **GBP 330 million** over **seven years**. ([Digital Health](https://www.digitalhealth.net/2023/11/nhs-england-awards-330m-federated-data-platform-contract-to-palantir/))

### Scope and Deliverables

The FDP is a software platform designed to:
- Sit across NHS trusts and Integrated Care Systems (ICSs)
- Connect data each organisation already holds in a "secure and safe environment"
- Operate as a **federated** system -- each organisation has its own instance that connects to a wider federation
- Cover real-time data: bed counts, elective waiting lists, staff rosters, medical supply availability, social care places
- Enable data-driven operational decisions across the entire NHS

### Backlash and Transparency Issues

The contract has been deeply controversial:

- **Redaction scandal:** Of the 586-page contract published by NHS England, **417 pages were completely blanked out**. Almost the entirety of the four pages on "Protection of Personal Data" were redacted. ([Digital Health](https://www.digitalhealth.net/2024/01/nhse-publishes-heavily-redacted-palantir-fdp-contract/))

- **Good Law Project legal action:** Filed suit arguing that "these full page redactions are unlawful and flout the Government's transparency guidelines as no reasoning is given behind them -- in line with the Freedom of Information Act." ([Good Law Project](https://goodlawproject.org/case/were-taking-legal-action-to-uncover-palantirs-blanked-out-contract/))

- **Low adoption:** Fewer than a quarter of England's 215 hospital trusts were actively using the FDP by end of 2024. ([Palantir NHS platform rejected by most hospitals](https://democracyforsale.substack.com/p/palantirs-nhs-data-platform-rejected-hospitals))

- **British Medical Association opposition:** Raised concerns about public trust and data protection implications.

- **Procurement concerns:** Critics argued the process was "flawed, secretive, and uncompetitive, with a poorly defined scope that raised the risk of uncontrolled and spiralling costs." ([Pharmaphorum](https://pharmaphorum.com/news/concerns-voiced-palantir-wins-ps330m-nhs-data-contract))

### What This Reveals About Deliverables

The NHS FDP contract is notable because the actual deliverable specifications remain largely secret due to redactions. What is publicly known suggests the SOW covers: platform licensing, data integration across trusts, federated architecture deployment, training for NHS staff, and ongoing operational support -- but the concrete KPIs, SLA penalties, and acceptance criteria are hidden behind 417 blank pages.

## 6. TITAN Program

### What Is TITAN?

TITAN stands for **Tactical Intelligence Targeting Access Node**. It is the Army's next-generation intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance (ISR) ground station that uses AI/ML to rapidly process sensor data from space, high-altitude, aerial, and terrestrial layers. It is a key program for the Army's Joint All Domain Command and Control (JADC2) concept.

### Contract Details

- **Award:** March 2024, $178.4M for Prototype Maturation Phase
- **Deliverables:** 10 prototype ground stations (5 "advanced" connecting to space assets, 5 "basic" tactical systems)
- **Performance period:** 24 months
- **Expected total acquisition:** 100-150 TITAN units
- **Total program value:** Expected to reach $823M+
- Palantir beat L3Harris in the competition

([Breaking Defense](https://breakingdefense.com/2024/03/palantir-wins-contract-for-army-titan-next-gen-targeting-system/); [DefenseScoop](https://defensescoop.com/2024/03/06/palantir-army-titan-ground-station-award-178-million/))

## 7. Ukraine Deployment

### Timeline

- **June 1, 2022:** CEO Alex Karp crossed from Poland into Ukraine on foot, met President Zelensky, offered to open a Kyiv office and deploy Palantir software for defense.
- **Summer 2022:** Initial deployment began. Palantir hired Ukrainian engineers to adapt software for wartime operations.
- **September 2022:** Ukraine had struck 400+ Russian targets with HIMARS, with Palantir Gotham central to the targeting process.
- **2022-2023:** MetaConstellation tool deployed -- orchestrating swarms of commercial satellites to provide near real-time battlefield pictures.
- **May 2023:** Ministry of Digital Transformation signed memorandum of cooperation covering defense applications and post-war reconstruction.
- **2024:** Partnership with Ministry of Economy for AI-powered demining at scale.
- **January 2026:** Ukraine reportedly feeding sensitive military data to Palantir AI for training purposes.

### Free vs. Paid

The deployment followed Palantir's classic **land-and-expand** pattern:
- Initial deployment appears to have been **provided at no cost or heavily subsidized** (Karp's offer to Zelensky; the UK "Homes for Ukraine" scheme also started with free Palantir services before converting to GBP 4.5M paid contract).
- Subsequent agreements transitioned to **formal government partnerships and commercial arrangements**.

### What Was Deployed

- **Gotham platform:** Core targeting and intelligence fusion
- **MetaConstellation:** Satellite tasking and commercial imagery orchestration
- **AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform):** AI-driven analysis of battlefield data

Karp claimed: "We are responsible for most of the targeting in Ukraine" -- though this is difficult to independently verify.

([TIME](https://time.com/6293398/palantir-future-of-warfare-ukraine/); [TIME AI War Lab](https://time.com/6691662/ai-ukraine-war-palantir/); [Defense News](https://www.defensenews.com/global/europe/2026/01/21/ukraine-feeds-sensitive-military-data-to-palantir-ai-for-training/))

## 8. Contract Structure Patterns -- What Do Government Contracts Actually Specify?

### The "Acquire, Expand, Scale" Model

Palantir's government contracting follows a deliberate three-phase pattern:

1. **Acquire:** Free/subsidized pilot or bootcamp. Palantir bears the cost. 5-day immersive "AIP boot camps" deliver real workflows on customer data. Often results in initial contract of $1-5M.

2. **Expand:** Additional use cases, workflows, and data domains layered in. Forward Deployed Software Engineers (FDSEs) embed with the customer -- focusing exclusively on one client, building production-ready workflows. Contracts grow to $10-50M range.

3. **Scale:** Enterprise-wide deployment. Sole-source justification becomes established. Contracts reach $100M-$1B+ range. Example: Army Vantage grew from initial engagement to $618.9M ceiling.

By 2025, Palantir had conducted over **1,500 AIP bootcamps**, each representing a potential multi-million dollar contract.

([FourWeekMBA](https://fourweekmba.com/palantir-business-model/); [GTM Foundry](https://www.gtmfoundry.vc/p/palantirs-bootcamp-gtm-strategy))

### What Government SOWs Typically Specify

Based on publicly available contract documents (e.g., the [ICE FALCON contract](https://www.ice.gov/doclib/foia/contracts/palantirTechHSCETC15C00001.pdf) and the USCG COVID-19 response contract), Palantir government SOWs typically include:

- **Term software licenses** (platform access, not perpetual licenses)
- **Data integration services** (connecting customer data sources into the platform)
- **Configuration of applications** (visualization, analysis, modeling)
- **Training:** Training customer engineers on Palantir's technical elements, training technical and non-technical users, leading training sessions
- **Professional services:** Working with subject matter experts to model data, supporting client business processes
- **Cloud platform provision:** FedRAMP-certified cloud-based environment
- **O&M (Operations & Maintenance):** Ongoing support services and custom enhancements

### What SOWs Do NOT Typically Specify

Notably absent from publicly visible contract language:
- **Specific FDE hours or headcount commitments** (Palantir controls staffing at its discretion)
- **Ontology scope definitions** (the ontology is built iteratively, not specified upfront)
- **Bootcamp as a formal deliverable** (bootcamps are pre-contract acquisition tools)
- **Source code delivery** (platform is SaaS; customer does not receive source code)
- **Data portability guarantees** (a major point of contention -- see Section 9)

## 9. Recompete Dynamics and Vendor Lock-In

### Has Anyone Won a Recompete vs. Palantir?

The short answer is: **essentially no, not at the federal level**. Palantir's contract history shows a pattern of:

1. **Winning initial competitions** -- e.g., beating Raytheon for DCGS-A ($800M contract, 2019) after suing the Army for the right to compete. ([Defense News](https://www.defensenews.com/land/2019/03/29/palantir-who-successfully-sued-the-army-just-won-a-major-army-contract/))

2. **Converting to sole-source follow-ons** -- Army Vantage was renewed as a sole-source contract. The Army filed "Justifications & Approvals for Other than Full and Open Competition" citing Palantir's proprietary rights. ([GovCDOiq](https://govcdoiq.org/info-bytes/procurements/army-intends-to-sole-source-619m-vantage-army-data-platform-to-palantir/))

3. **Consolidating into enterprise agreements** -- The $10B Army ESA consolidates 75 existing contracts, making future recompetition structurally unlikely.

### The Lock-In Mechanism

Michael Burry (the investor famous for "The Big Short") described Palantir's moat as "obstruction of data transfer." ([Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/02/50660704/palantirs-moat-is-just-obstruction-of-data-transfer-michael-burry-says))

Specific evidence of lock-in:
- **NYPD case:** After years of use, the NYPD claimed they could not access the analytical insights and "tags" their own investigators had generated within the software. Palantir argued that while the customer owns the raw data, the way the software organizes, links, and visualizes data is Palantir's intellectual property.
- **FDE embedding:** Forward-deployed engineers create deep dependency -- the agency becomes reliant on Palantir personnel who understand the custom-built workflows.
- **Proprietary ontology:** The data model (ontology) built within Palantir is not easily portable to competing platforms.

### Palantir Also Challenges Others' Sole-Source Awards

In July 2025, Palantir challenged the DIA's sole-source contract plan to another vendor, arguing federal acquisition laws require competition -- echoing its own 2016 court victory against the Army. ([Washington Technology](https://www.washingtontechnology.com/contracts/2025/07/palantir-challenges-dias-sole-source-contract-plan/406477/))

## 10. FedRAMP / IL5 / IL6 Implications

### Current Certification Status

Palantir holds:
- **FedRAMP High Baseline Authorization** (granted 2024) ([Palantir IR](https://investors.palantir.com/news-details/2024/Palantir-Granted-FedRAMP-High-Baseline-Authorization/))
- **DoD Impact Level 5 (IL5)** Provisional Authorization
- **DoD Impact Level 6 (IL6)** Provisional Authorization -- one of only **six cloud service providers** to achieve IL6
- **CMMC Level 2** certification (September 2025)
- **PFCS Forward** -- DISA authorized extension of IL5/IL6 to on-premises and edge deployments (February 2026)

### How This Affects Contract Structure

The "**authorize once, use many**" model is transformative for procurement:

1. **Eliminates per-site accreditation:** PFCS Forward provides a Provisional Authorization package that is inherited by every end customer. Agencies using PFCS Forward skip site-specific implementation, documentation, and assessment of software security controls.

2. **Hardware-agnostic deployment:** A single accreditation package works from large data center deployments to "a small form factor designed to be mobile and survivable in the back of a vehicle."

3. **Procurement fast-tracking:** IL6 authorization means agencies can bypass lengthy bespoke audit and accreditation processes. This dramatically reduces the time from contract award to operational deployment.

4. **Competitive moat:** With only 6 CSPs holding IL6, the competitive field for classified DoD contracts is inherently limited. This is a structural barrier to entry that makes recompetition against Palantir even harder.

([Palantir Blog](https://blog.palantir.com/how-palantir-meets-il6-security-requirements-with-apollo-f6c3f0c51fe); [BusinessWire PFCS Forward](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260212887851/en/Palantir-Receives-DISA-Authorization-for-PFCS-Forward-Extending-IL5-and-IL6-Accreditation-to-On-Premises-and-Edge-Deployments))

## 12. Summary of Key Findings

### What Palantir Actually Promises to Deliver (Based on Public Evidence)

| Deliverable Category | Evidence Source | Typical SOW Language |
|---------------------|----------------|---------------------|
| Platform licensing (SaaS) | All contracts | "Term software licenses," "Software-as-a-Service" |
| Data integration | ICE, USCG, Vantage | "Integrate data sets of any size or format," "180+ data sources" |
| AI/ML capabilities | Maven, TITAN | "AI-driven tools for advanced target management," "AI algorithms and machine learning" |
| Professional services | G-Cloud, USCG | "Implementation and Engineering Services per person per quarter," "Solution design consultancy per two-week sprint" |
| Training | G-Cloud, USCG, all | "Training customer engineers," "instructor-led," "self-paced e-learning" |
| Cloud infrastructure | FedRAMP contracts | "FedRAMP certified cloud-based platform" |
| Prototype hardware | TITAN | "10 prototype ground stations" (5 advanced, 5 basic) |
| O&M support | ICE, Vantage | "Operations and Maintenance support services and custom enhancements" |

### What Is NOT in Public SOW Language

- No mention of **specific FDE headcount or hours** committed
- No mention of **bootcamp deliverables** (these are pre-contract sales tools)
- No mention of **ontology scope** or data model specifications
- No mention of **data portability** guarantees or exit provisions
- **SLA terms** are consistently described as "available on request" rather than published
- The most sensitive contract sections (data protection, KPIs, penalties) are systematically **redacted** in published versions

### The Palantir Contracting Playbook

1. Enter via bootcamp or subsidized pilot (often free)
2. Embed FDSEs to build custom workflows on proprietary platform
3. Prove value; expand use cases and data domains
4. Achieve sole-source status through proprietary lock-in
5. Consolidate multiple contracts into enterprise agreements
6. Leverage security certifications (IL5/IL6/FedRAMP) as competitive moat
7. Repeat at larger scale across agencies and allied nations

This pattern -- visible across U.S. Army, NHS, UK MoD, Space Force, and ICE -- is the most honest window into what Palantir actually delivers: not a product you buy, but a **platform dependency you enter**.
