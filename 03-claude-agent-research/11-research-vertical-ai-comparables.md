## 1. VERTICAL AI SAAS THAT REACHED $10M+ ARR SERVING SMBs

### Harvey (Legal AI)
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Founded:** 2022
- **Funding:** $500M+ total (Series D led by a16z at $8B valuation, Dec 2025; talks for $11B in Feb 2026)
- **Revenue:** $195M ARR (end 2025), up 3.9x from $50M ARR at end 2024. Hit $100M in ~36 months
- **Pricing:** Enterprise contracts; largest customer pays $4M+/year; ~$40K revenue/employee
- **Target:** Am Law 200 firms, expanding to enterprise legal departments (Comcast, Verizon)
- **Time to $10M ARR:** ~18 months from launch (mid-2022 to late 2023)
- **Spider relevance:** Vertical AI playbook executed at hyperspeed. Proves domain-specific AI can command premium pricing. However, targets large enterprises, not SMBs. Growth rate (3.9x YoY) is the benchmark to beat.
- **Sources:** [Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/harvey/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/12/04/legal-ai-startup-harvey-confirms-8b-valuation/), [Latka](https://getlatka.com/blog/harvey-revenue-valuation/)

### Vanta (Compliance/Security)
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Founded:** 2018
- **Funding:** $504M total across 4 rounds; $4.15B valuation (Series D, Jul 2025)
- **Revenue:** $220M ARR (Jul 2025), up from $152M in 2024. 8,000+ customers
- **Pricing:** Core: $10K/year (1 framework); Plus: $15-30K; Growth: $30K+; Scale: up to $80K. Translates to ~$833-$6,667/month -- DIRECTLY COMPARABLE TO SPIDER'S EUR 500-5,000/mo
- **Target:** Startups and mid-market needing SOC 2, ISO 27001, HIPAA compliance
- **Time to $10M ARR:** ~3 years (2018-2021). Built to $10M ARR on seed funding alone before raising Series A
- **Spider relevance:** CLOSEST PRICING COMPARABLE. Vanta proves you can build a $220M ARR business selling $10-30K/year contracts to mid-market companies. Product-led growth into compliance (a regulated, complex domain). 25% of revenue now international. Their "wait until $10M ARR to raise Series A" is a powerful bootstrapping lesson.
- **Sources:** [SaaStr](https://www.saastr.com/the-early-days-5-things-vanta-got-right-and-5-it-got-wrong-getting-to-first-10m-arr/), [Sacra](https://sacra.com/research/vanta-at-220m-year/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/07/23/crowdstrike-backed-vanta-is-valued-at-4-billion-in-new-funding-round.html)

### EvenUp (Legal AI -- Personal Injury)
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Founded:** 2019
- **Funding:** $385M total; $2B+ valuation (Series E led by Bessemer, Oct 2025)
- **Revenue:** ARR doubling YoY; 2,000+ law firms; 90% of 2025 new sales from product expansions
- **Pricing:** Value-based; largest customer pays $4M+/year. Outcome-aligned pricing
- **Target:** Personal injury law firms (top 100 US PI firms: 20% are customers)
- **Spider relevance:** Bessemer portfolio company. Proves the "AI-enabled services" model -- replacing labor budgets, not just software budgets. TAM expansion beyond traditional software TAM. Key lesson: start narrow (demand letters), expand into full workflow.
- **Sources:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/10/07/exclusive-evenup-raises-150-million-series-e-at-2-billion-valuation-as-ai-reshapes-personal-injury-law/), [EvenUp Blog](https://www.evenuplaw.com/blog/evenup-2b-valuation/)

### Ironclad (Legal/Contract AI)
- **HQ:** San Francisco, CA
- **Founded:** 2014
- **Funding:** ~$333M total; $3.2B valuation
- **Revenue:** $150M ARR (Jan 2025), up 39% YoY
- **Pricing:** Enterprise SaaS; mid-market plans likely $2-5K/month
- **Target:** Enterprise and mid-market legal teams

### Ramp (Finance/Spend Management)
- **HQ:** New York, NY
- **Founded:** 2019
- **Funding:** $1.6B+ total; $32B valuation (Dec 2025)
- **Revenue:** $1B+ annualized revenue (Sep 2025). Reached $100M run rate in ~2 years

- **Time to $10M ARR:** ~12-18 months (fastest in cohort)
- **Spider relevance:** Fintech model (interchange revenue) not directly comparable, but their speed to scale in SMB and aggressive multi-product expansion (10+ products, each hitting $1M ARR within 5-6 months) is the operational playbook to study.
- **Sources:** [Contrary Research](https://research.contrary.com/company/ramp), [Ramp Press](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/ramp-reaches-32-billion-valuation-doubling-revenue-and-customers-in-past-year-302616510.html)

## 3. "PALANTIR FOR SMBs" ATTEMPTS

### Palantir's Own SMB Push: Foundry for Builders
- **What happened:** Palantir officially launched "Palantir for SMBs" and "Palantir for Builders" to reach startups and smaller companies
- **Pricing:** Usage-based on 3 dimensions: Compute (compute-seconds), Storage (GB-months), Ontology (GB-months). Specific pricing not public
- **Status:** Active but limited traction with true SMBs. Platform complexity remains a barrier. Primarily attracts well-funded startups, not traditional SMBs
- **Spider relevance:** VALIDATES THE THESIS. Palantir itself recognizes the SMB opportunity but struggles to serve it due to platform complexity and enterprise DNA. This is Spider's exact wedge: Palantir-grade capability with SMB-grade simplicity and pricing.
- **Sources:** [Palantir for SMBs](https://www.palantir.com/offerings/palantir-for-builders/SMBs/), [Palantir for Builders](https://www.palantir.com/offerings/palantir-for-builders/)

- **Notable alumni startups:** Anduril ($12B valuation), Clarent (senior living), various healthcare/defense
- **15% went through YC**

- **Sources:** [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91480910/9-startups-from-palantir-alumni-you-need-to-know), [Sifted](https://sifted.eu/articles/palantir-founders-startups-europe), [Nocturnalknight](https://nocturnalknight.co/inside-the-palantir-mafia-startups-that-are-quietly-shaping-the-future/)

### Bessemer's 4 Assessment Dimensions for Vertical AI
1. **Functional value** -- Does the AI solve a real problem better than humans/software?
2. **Economic value** -- Does it access labor budgets (not just IT budgets)?
3. **Competitive dynamics** -- Is the vertical defensible?
4. **Defensibility** -- Can the company build data moats?

### Bessemer's AI Pricing Playbook
- **Key insight:** "Your charge metric is a strategic statement, not just a billing decision"
- **Winning model:** Hybrid pricing (base subscription + usage/outcome tiers)
- **Tokens** work for technical buyers but confuse everyone else
- **Outcomes** maximize value alignment but require absorbing cost variability
- **Gross margins:** AI companies see 50-60% initially, maturing to 60-70% (vs. 80-90% for traditional SaaS)
- **92% of AI software companies** now use mixed/hybrid pricing models
- **Spider implication:** EUR 500-5,000/month base + usage-based compute/queries is the right model. Aim for 65%+ gross margin. The hybrid model generates the highest median growth (21% faster than pure subscription or pure usage).

| European expansion countries | FR -> DACH -> UK -> Benelux | Agicap/Qonto/Spendesk pattern |

### Strategic Lessons

1. **Start narrow, expand wide (EvenUp model):** Begin with one killer use case (e.g., cash flow analytics for PMEs), then expand into full operations. EvenUp started with demand letters, now does full case management.

2. **"Wait for $10M ARR" fundraising (Vanta model):** Vanta built to $10M ARR on seed money. This gives negotiating leverage and proves unit economics. Spider could do the same with EUR 500K-1M initial funding.

3. **Hybrid pricing is optimal (Bessemer data):** Base subscription (EUR 500/mo minimum) + usage-based compute. This provides revenue predictability while capturing upside as customers use more AI features.

4. **European SMB AI adoption is at inflection point:** From 15% to 31% GenAI adoption in one year (2023-2024). The market is ready NOW. French government backing (EUR 10B via Bpifrance) creates tailwinds.

5. **No "Palantir for SMBs" exists yet:** Palantir alumni build defense tech and enterprise tools. Palantir's own SMB push struggles. DualEntry/Campfire are US-focused and finance-only. The European cross-vertical operations AI platform does not exist.

6. **Compound startup model (Rippling playbook):** Build a "system of record" (data ontology), then launch multiple product lines on top. Each new product should hit EUR 1M ARR within 6 months of launch.

7. **France-first, then DACH:** Agicap, Qonto, and Spendesk all started in France, then expanded to Germany. This is the proven European SMB expansion path. Personio did it in reverse (Germany first).
